# Executive Summary

Phishing attacks remain one of the most common and dangerous cyber threats targeting individuals, businesses, and organizations worldwide. Attackers use social engineering techniques, spoofed email addresses, fake websites, and malicious attachments to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking credentials, and confidential business data.

This project focuses on identifying and analyzing phishing emails, understanding attacker techniques, classifying risks, and creating cybersecurity awareness documentation.

The project involved:
- Analysis of phishing and suspicious email samples
- Email header inspection
- URL and domain analysis
- Risk classification
- Awareness and prevention documentation

The goal was to simulate real-world phishing analysis and demonstrate practical cybersecurity investigation techniques.

---

# Objectives

The primary objectives of this project were:

- Identify phishing indicators in email samples
- Analyze suspicious email headers
- Detect spoofed domains and malicious links
- Classify emails based on risk level
- Create employee awareness documentation

# Methodology

The following methodology was followed during this project:

1. Collection of publicly available phishing email samples.
2. Identification of phishing indicators such as spoofed domains, urgent language, suspicious attachments, and malicious links.
3. Analysis of email headers using online header analysis tools.
4. Classification of email samples into Safe, Suspicious, and Phishing categories.
5. Documentation of findings and creation of awareness materials.

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Spoofed Domains
Several phishing emails used domains visually similar to legitimate organizations.

Example:
- paypa1-support.com
- micr0soft-security.com

---

## 2. Social Engineering Techniques
Attackers used fear, urgency, and account suspension warnings to manipulate users.

---

## 3. Malicious URLs
Phishing emails redirected users to fake login portals designed to steal credentials.

---

## 4. Suspicious Attachments
Compressed ZIP attachments were identified as potential malware delivery mechanisms.

---

## 5. Header Analysis Results
Email header analysis revealed:
- SPF failures
- Missing DKIM signatures
- Suspicious relay servers
- Sender spoofing indicators

# Recommendations

The following cybersecurity measures are recommended:

- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Verify sender email domains
- Avoid clicking unknown links
- Regularly conduct employee awareness training
- Use email filtering and anti-phishing protection
- Report suspicious emails immediately
- Keep systems and browsers updated
